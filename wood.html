Woodworking Projects

Texas Inventionworks, the University of Texas at Austin's makerspace, has a woodshop with an abundance of woodworking machines including, but not limited to, a table saw, miter saw, routing table, jointer, planer, and belt sander. These projects, specifically the cutting board, were created with the purpose of making an instructable, much like my work in the machine shop, to encourage students to utilize TIW's facilities.

End Grain Cutting Board

Summary: I've always procrastinated in making my parents a Mother's and Father's Day gift, so in 2025, I decided to start early. They're very practical people, so I decided to make them something they would use every day. This project also gave me the opportunity to create an instructable I could share with the hundreds of students that visit Texas Inventionworks.

Design Process: A simple YouTube video helped me understand the basic intuition and purpose of an end-grain cutting board. Following a minimalistic approach, I opted to use Maple for the entire board. After making calculations to determine the necessary strip length and thickness, I purchased my wood and began working.

Skills: Woodworking, Material Selection

Desk Riser (IN PROGRESS)

Summary: My monitor takes up a large portion of my desk, which takes up space I would much rather dedicate to homework, class notes, or my sketchbook of ideas. I also hoped to develop my woodworking skills with some joinery to give me more confidence in my upcoming woodshop project.

Design Process: Like much of my other projects, I started with a sketch then scrolled through Etsy and Pinterest to find a final design. Although I typically like more minimalistic designs, I opted to create two shelves for my laptop hub and charging station. During the CAD process, I made sure to use parameters and relative measurements for each aspect of the desk riser to ensure I could use this same model to visualize potentially making a desk riser as a gift to a friend.

Skills: Woodworking, Joinery
